Bio

Pearce Ritchie is a composer and bass player who draws from years of playing classical and jazz music of many different styles along with an interest in computers to write music that explores an eclectic array of sounds and playing styles. He is interested in fully live music; but is also works with the possibilities available when incorporating prerecorded audio into performances, as well as creating holistic experiences captured with recordings.

Pearce has gone on to study music with an AFA in Music from Normandale Community College and a BA in Music Theory and Composition from the University of Minnesota Duluth. He is currently pursuing a MM in Music Composition & Theory at the University of Wisconsin Milwaukee. His double bass teachers include Bob Anderson and Gordon Lewis, and music composition include Justin Rubin and Nathaniel Haering.

Pearce’s compositions have been performed by the UWM New Music Ensemble and the UMD Percussion Ensemble; and he has self released an EP Late Night Snack. His works include a piece for 3 percussionists and fixed media Glass Chatters, as well as a multi movement piece for string quartet The Earthworms Rainy Delight and a collection of bass duets. His current projects include a piece exploring continuous digital audio decay processes capturing the transformation cause by audio compression algorithms.
