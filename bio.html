Bio

Pearce Ritchie is a composer and bass player who draws from years of playing classical and jazz music to write music that explores cyclical forms and rhythms. He is interested in fully live music, the possibilities available with incorporating pre-recorded audio into performances, as well as creating holistic experiences captured with recordings.

Starting with playing trombone in his elementary school concert band, Pearce later explored jazz and contemporary music with bass. Pearce has gone on to study music with an AFA in Music from Normandale Community College and is currently completing a BA in Music Theory and Composition from the University of Minnesota Duluth. He has studied double bass with Bob Anderson and Gordon Lewis, and music composition with Justin Rubin.

Pearce has self released an EP Late Night Snack, a piece for 3 percussionists and fixed media Glass Chatters, as well as having written pieces for string quartet and piano. His current projects include a multi movement piece for string quartet The Earthworms Rainy Delight and a collection of bass duets.
